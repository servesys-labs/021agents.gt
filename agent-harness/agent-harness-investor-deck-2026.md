# Agent Harness
## Managed Agents For The Operational Middle

A Cloudflare-native managed agent platform for SMB support and operations.

- SMBs need more than a consumer assistant, but less than an enterprise agent transformation program.
- Agent Harness is designed to become the managed operating layer for support, internal workflows, and lightweight business automation.
- The product already shows unusual pre-MVP depth: multi-tenant runtime, browser tools, sandboxed code execution, MCP connectivity, semantic memory, channels, billing, and eval scaffolding.

---

# The Opportunity
## SMBs need a system for support and ops, not just another chatbot

- Consumer AI products are optimized for individuals, not for running repeatable support and operations workflows across a business.
- Enterprise agent suites are powerful, but they are expensive, implementation-heavy, and designed for larger organizations with longer buying cycles.
- Vertical support agents monetize well, but they leave adjacent workflows like back-office automation, research, and internal knowledge work fragmented.
- The market gap is the operational middle: SMB teams that want one managed agent system for both customer-facing and internal workflows.

# Product Foundation
## More platform depth than a typical pre-MVP

- Multi-tenant Durable Object runtime with WebSocket sessions, agent supervision, and per-agent model selection.
- Tool layer for browser automation, screenshots, search, code execution, previews, git workflows, API/database calls, and persistent workspaces.
- Memory stack with context blocks, semantic memory through Vectorize, and skill overlays that make agents more stateful and useful over time.
- Business surfaces already present in the gateway and UI: billing, credits, sessions, marketplace, connectors, voice, channels, eval runs, and RAG document handling.

# Beachhead Market
## Start with support and operations teams under 100 employees

- Ideal customers are SMB SaaS companies, ecommerce operators, agencies, and support-heavy teams that cannot justify a heavyweight enterprise platform.
- Initial workflows include support deflection, internal knowledge retrieval, repeated back-office automation, lead triage, and lightweight research or coding tasks.
- The likely buyer is a founder, support lead, RevOps leader, or operations owner who wants one system rather than multiple narrow AI tools.
- Expansion motion is simple: once support and ops are live, add more workflows without asking the customer to adopt a new platform.

# Business Model
## Subscription plus usage aligns with both the product and the market

| Plan | Target customer | Suggested price | Included scope |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Starter | Founder-led SMB or small support team | `$199-$299/month` | 2-3 seats, 3 agents, web or email support, included monthly credits |
| Growth | SMB with recurring support and ops workflows | `$799-$999/month` | 5-10 seats, more agents, voice, sandbox, evals, larger credit pool |
| Scale | Multi-team mid-market customer | `$2,000+/month` or annual commit | SSO, custom connectors, observability, support SLAs, contracted usage |

- Support workflows can layer in `$0.60-$0.90` per resolved conversation once automation quality is established.
- Internal ops, research, and coding flows are better billed through credits plus premium model pass-through.
- This keeps entry pricing simple while preserving margin as automation depth increases.
